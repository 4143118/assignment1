Interactive Experience Research

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

I chose the website gehry.getty.edu. When I first interacted with the experience, the first thing I noticed was a prompt that said “click anywhere to enable sound.” I noticed it because it moved along with the cursor. This real-time feedback immediately attracted my attention and encouraged me to interact with the page.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent a lot of time engaging with the animated text interaction on the homepage. Because it occupies half of the page and is the only moving element on the page, my attention stayed on this part most of the time. It also produces real-time deformation effect when the mouse moves up and down over it. In addition, it creates a text trail effect along the path of the cursor.

Because a single sliding interaction produced two effects, I wanted to see whether it had more interactive effects. So I clicked on this part and discovered that it also produced real-time feedback in the form of background colour changes and further deformation. Then, in order to verify whether the background colour changed to a fixed colour or to different colours, I clicked on the animated text multiple times. After realizing that the background colour changes randomly between red, blue, and green, I became interested in the maximum degree of the font deformation. So I clicked and held the text, and found that the text could eventually occupy the entire page. Because this part contains many interaction functions, I spent a lot of time on it.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was scrolling down the page. Because this website contains a large amount of information, and most of its content is presented on a single scrolling page, I had to scroll down frequently in order to view more information.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the Interactive experience?

I think the primary goal of this website is to tell the story behind how Frank Gehry and his team designed the Walt Disney Concert Hall. It aims to introduce visitors to the factors that need to be considered in architectural design and to record this design process in the form of a digital archive.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

This interactive experience combines videos, models, explanatory text, explanatory images, sketches, and images for archive to help users understand the process of how the building developed from concept to completion. Instead of simply presenting all the information directly to visitors, it combines different interactions and uses multiple forms of materials as support to help users gradually understand the process.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think this design mainly serves a single, long using session. However, for people who are interested in architecture, especially the building mentioned here, the website encourages multiple and extended visits.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interaction experience of this website is structured like a journey rather than repeatedly jumping between different pages. Therefore, ordinary users usually use the website in a focused way. However, for architecture enthusiasts, it also provides a clear menu function that allows users to quickly jump to different chapters, which encourages repeated use of the website.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website references several media forms. The font design and layout on the homepage remind me of poster design. In addition, the layout of the text and images in the following sections also reminds me of magazine layouts.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Just as I mentioned earlier about the journey storytelling structure, the magazine-inspired layout encourages users to look through the website from beginning to end. In addition, it encourages users to carefully observe the images and treat them as support for the text.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Because magazines give me the impression of having a high aesthetic quality and being artistic, when I looking through this website I tend to appreciate it carefully. In addition, since the website learns the mature layout style of magazines, I feel pleased by the aesthetic quality of the design while using it.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

I think there is no particular part of this website that feels especially frustrating to me. Although there is sometimes a bit of lag, considering that this is a single-page website with very rich interactions, the slight lag is acceptable.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The transition animations between sections are the most satisfying part for me. The transitions are presented in many different forms, so it does not feel like I am just browsing a boring single page website, but more like scrolling through short videos such as TikTok. The website uses scrolling instead of page turning, and uses interesting visual effects to deliver a feeling of transitions between different pieces of content.
